Getting Started Guide

Last Updated: February 2026

Welcome to Mind the Gap! This guide will help you get started with our psychology-backed study platform in just a few minutes.

1. Take the Diagnostic

Your journey begins with our 12-question cognitive assessment. This research-backed diagnostic takes just 2 minutes and reveals how your brain learns best.

What we measure:

  • Your preferred learning modality (visual, auditory, kinesthetic)
  • Peak concentration times throughout the day
  • Information processing speed and retention patterns
  • Attention span and focus duration
  • Memory formation preferences

2. Explore Your Personalised Hub

Once you complete the diagnostic, you'll receive access to your unique study hub—one of 700 possible configurations tailored specifically to your cognitive profile.

Your hub includes:

  • Custom Study Resources: Materials formatted for your learning style
  • Optimized Schedule: Study sessions timed to your peak hours
  • Active Recall Tools: Flashcards and quizzes designed for your retention patterns
  • Progress Analytics: Visual dashboards showing your improvement over time

3. Meet Your AI Study Partner

Your hub comes with 24/7 AI support that understands your unique learning profile. Think of it as a study buddy who never sleeps and always knows exactly what you need.

Your AI can:

  • Send personalised reminders based on your schedule
  • Answer questions about your study materials
  • Suggest when to take breaks based on your focus patterns
  • Celebrate your streak milestones and achievements

4. Build Your Study Streak

Consistency is key to learning. Our accountability system helps you build daily study habits that stick.

Students who maintain a 21-day streak see an average grade improvement of 23%. Start small—even 15 minutes counts!

Need Help?

If you run into any issues or have questions, reach out to our support team. We typically respond within 24 hours.

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: February 1, 2026

At Mind the Gap, we take your privacy seriously. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information.

Information We Collect

Diagnostic Data

When you complete our 12-question cognitive assessment, we collect your responses to generate your personalised learning profile. This data is:

  • Encrypted during transmission and storage
  • Never sold to third parties
  • Used solely to optimise your study experience
  • Anonymized for research purposes

Study Activity

We track your study sessions, completion rates, and progress to provide analytics and improve your learning outcomes. This includes:

  • Time spent studying
  • Topics covered and materials accessed
  • Quiz and flashcard performance
  • Streak maintenance and goal completion

Account Information

We collect basic account details including your name, email address, school affiliation (if applicable), and year of study.

How We Use Your Information

Your data helps us:

  • Personalise your study hub and recommendations
  • Send relevant notifications and reminders
  • Improve our platform through anonymized research
  • Provide customer support
  • Share aggregated insights with partner schools (no individual data)

Data Sharing & School Partnerships

For students at partner schools, we may share aggregated, anonymized data with your teachers and school administrators. This helps improve classroom learning but never reveals individual student performance without explicit consent.

We never share:

  • Individual student data without consent
  • Personal information with advertisers
  • Diagnostic results with third parties

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of all data we hold about you
  • Deletion: Ask us to delete your account and all associated data
  • Correction: Update or correct any inaccurate information
  • Opt-Out: Disable certain data collection features

Data Security

We use industry-standard security measures including:

  • End-to-end encryption for sensitive data
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Secure cloud storage with redundant backups
  • Two-factor authentication for account access

Cookies & Tracking

We use essential cookies to maintain your session and preferences. We do not use third-party advertising cookies or trackers.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy periodically. Material changes will be communicated via email and posted on this page with an updated effective date.

GDPR Compliance

Effective Date: February 1, 2026

Mind the Gap is committed to protecting your personal data in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). As an Irish-based company serving students across the EU, we take data protection seriously.

Your GDPR Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right to Access

You can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you. This includes your diagnostic results, study history, and account information. We will provide this data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format within 30 days.

Right to Rectification

If any of your personal data is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to have it corrected. You can update most information directly through your account settings, or contact us for assistance.

Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

You can request deletion of your personal data at any time. We will permanently delete your account and all associated data within 30 days, except where we have a legal obligation to retain certain information.

Right to Restrict Processing

You can request that we limit how we process your data in certain circumstances, such as when you contest the accuracy of your data or object to processing.

Right to Data Portability

You can receive your personal data in a portable format and transmit it to another service provider. We provide data exports in JSON and CSV formats.

Right to Object

You can object to processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes or where we process your data based on legitimate interests.

Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making

You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing that significantly affects you. While our platform uses AI for personalised recommendations, all study plans can be manually customised.

Lawful Basis for Processing

We process your data under the following legal bases:

  • Consent: You provide explicit consent when creating an account and completing the diagnostic
  • Contract: Processing is necessary to provide the services you've subscribed to
  • Legitimate Interests: We process data to improve our platform and provide customer support
  • Legal Obligation: We retain certain data to comply with financial and legal requirements

Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  • Identity Data: Name, username, date of birth
  • Contact Data: Email address, school affiliation
  • Diagnostic Data: Learning profile, cognitive assessment results
  • Usage Data: Study sessions, materials accessed, performance metrics
  • Technical Data: IP address, device type, browser information

Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as you maintain an active account. After account deletion:

  • Most data is immediately deleted
  • Some anonymized data may be retained for research and analytics
  • Financial records are retained for 7 years as required by Irish law
  • Backup copies are purged within 90 days

International Data Transfers

Your data is primarily stored on servers within the EU (Ireland and Germany). If we transfer data outside the EU, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place through:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission
  • Adequacy decisions for certain countries
  • Your explicit consent where required

Children's Data

For users under 16, we require verifiable parental consent before processing personal data. Parents and guardians can:

  • Review their child's data
  • Request corrections or deletions
  • Withdraw consent at any time
  • Access their child's study activity and progress

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will:

  • Notify the Irish Data Protection Commission within 72 hours
  • Inform affected users without undue delay
  • Provide details about the breach and steps taken to mitigate harm
